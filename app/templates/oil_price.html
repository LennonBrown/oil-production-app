What is WTI?

West Texas Intermediate is a grade of crude oil used as a benchmark in oil pricing. It is extracted in the US and traded on the NYMEX exchange.

🌎

Why does it matter?

WTI prices affect everything from petrol costs to airline tickets. A $10 rise in oil prices can add billions to global transport costs overnight.

📊

Data Source

Prices sourced live from the U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA) API — the same organisation behind our production dataset.
