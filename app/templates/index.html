Why Track Oil Production?

Oil is the world's most traded commodity. Its production levels ripple across every economy on Earth.

📈

Economic Indicator

Oil production directly sets energy prices worldwide. When major producers like Saudi Arabia or Russia increase output, prices fall globally — affecting transport, manufacturing, and household bills in every country.

🌟

OPEC & Geopolitics

The Organisation of Petroleum Exporting Countries (OPEC) controls roughly 40% of global supply. Their production decisions — made in Vienna — can trigger recessions or fuel economic booms thousands of miles away.

⛳

WTI & Brent Crude

West Texas Intermediate (WTI) and Brent Crude are the two benchmark prices that set the global oil market. Airlines, logistics firms, and governments watch these figures daily to plan budgets and hedge risk.

🌍

The Energy Transition

As renewables grow, understanding which nations remain dependent on oil exports is critical. Tracking production trends reveals which economies face the biggest challenges in the shift to clean energy.

Regional Overview
